Once upon a pond…

Somewhere past the last hiking trail there's a pond called Beaverville. The water's cold, the birch is crunchy, and nobody's in a hurry. It was a good life. Then somebody in a suspiciously soft hat showed up and started eyeing everyone's fur.

Then came the humans 😤

Word around the woods is that beaver fur makes a marvelously fine hat, and the humans know it. They rolled up with big scary nets and a hat catalogue, planning to turn the whole colony into next season's fashion. The beavers found the catalogue. The beavers are not pleased. The devil wears Prada, but he accessorizes with beaver.

Downstream, they built the thing the elders won't name after dark: the Processor. A great groaning water mill that spins river current into hat felt. As long as the river runs, it churns.

They put up posters 📜

This is The Rusty. Dam vandal, folk hero, chews through evidence and traps alike. The reward is forty shillings or one luxury hat, self-supplied, which tells you everything about who wrote it. Nobody has collected. Nobody will.

So: BUILD A DAM! 🪵

The Processor runs on the river. No river, no mill. No mill, no felt. No felt, no hats made of your neighbors. Chew down a tree, drag it home, wedge it in just right, and do it again about four hundred times until that mill wheel grinds to a stop and the hatters are fuming on a dry bank. It's honest work, and nobody here is becoming a hat.
